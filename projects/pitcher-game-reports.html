Pitcher Game Reports

This project focused on creating pitcher game reports to better visualize individual performances. The goal was to provide a tool that highlights how elite pitchers achieve their results. Using R to scrape game data, I built a script that filters, cleans, and analyzes the necessary information, then generates a series of plots to capture key aspects of a pitcher’s outing. The workflow began with a parameter-based filtering system to select pitchers and isolate their performance on a specific day. To improve readability, I also created a custom pitch “library” with consistent color coding across all visualizations. From there, the script produced several plots:
  • Movement plot – displays pitch movement, with point size scaled to usage, offering insight into a pitcher’s arsenal and movement profile.
  • Usage donut chart by handedness – shows how a pitcher’s arsenal and approach differ against left- and right-handed batters.
    • Usage donut chart by handedness – shows how a pitcher’s arsenal and approach differ against left- and right-handed batters.
  • Velocity boxplot – highlights velocity ranges for each pitch type to capture what the pitcher had working that day.
  • Strike zone command plot – visualizes pitch location patterns to reveal how pitchers attack the zone and where they prefer to throw.
  • Pitching line table – provides traditional box score context alongside the advanced visuals.
After generating these plots, I used a complementary Python script to assemble them into a single 1600x900 PNG image, making the reports easy to share on social media. Overall, this system produces concise, visually rich reports that allow users to quickly understand not just what results a pitcher achieved, but why they were successful—offering valuable perspective on what it takes to perform at the highest level.
